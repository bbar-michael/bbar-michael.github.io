History of the Internet

How It All Began

The Internet started as ARPANET in the late 1960s, a U.S. government project to connect computers across long distances. Over time, it evolved into today’s global network connecting billions of devices.

How the Internet Works

The Internet works by connecting devices through routers that direct traffic. Every device has an IP address, which acts like a unique street address for communication. Information travels in packets that move across bandwidth, the measure of how much data can flow through the network. Thanks to these systems, we can browse websites, stream videos, and send messages worldwide.

Key Vocabulary

  • IP Address – A unique number identifying a device on the network.
  • Router – A device that forwards data between networks.
  • Bandwidth – The maximum data transfer capacity of a network connection.
  • Packets – Small units of data transmitted across the network.
